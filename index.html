Cryptocurrency

Bitcoin is the world’s first decentralized digital currency, launched in 2009. It allows people to send and receive money securely without needing banks or intermediaries. Transactions are recorded on a public ledger called the blockchain, making it transparent and nearly impossible to hack. Unlike traditional currencies, Bitcoin has a limited supply of 21 million coins, which makes it valuable and often referred to as “digital gold.” Today, it is used for online payments, trading, and as an investment by millions worldwide.
